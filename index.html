The Rich Legacy of Mulmul Cotton Sarees

Mulmul, also known as muslin, has a storied history dating back to ancient India. Originally woven in the Bengal region, mulmul was prized for its fine, airy texture that felt like a gentle breeze against the skin. The fabric was so delicate and sheer that it was often referred to as the "woven air" by those who admired its ethereal quality.

Historically, mulmul cotton was reserved for royalty, with weavers painstakingly creating each saree by hand. These sarees were not only comfortable but also exuded an aura of sophistication and grace. Over time, mulmul cotton sarees became more accessible to the general public, and their popularity spread across India and beyond.

Caring for Your Mulmul Cotton Sarees

To ensure that your mulmul cotton sarees remain in pristine condition, it's important to follow proper care instructions. Here are some tips to help you maintain the quality and longevity of your sarees:

Gentle Washing: Mulmul cotton is a delicate fabric that should be washed with care. Hand wash your sarees in cold water using a mild detergent to prevent damage to the fibers. To prevent the cloth from losing its shape, do not twist or wring it.

Air Drying: After washing, allow your saree to air dry in a shaded area, away from direct sunlight. Sun exposure can cause the colors to fade over time. Gently spread the saree on a flat surface or hang it on a clothesline to dry.
